1. Filter data as early as possible: This rule can be applied when there are filtering conditions in the WHERE clause that can be moved to subqueries to reduce the amount of data processed in the later stages of the query.
2. Avoid unnecessary joins: This rule can be applied when there are joins in the query that do not contribute to the final result set, i.e., the joined table's columns are not used in the SELECT, WHERE, or GROUP BY clauses.
3. Use subqueries to reduce data size: This rule can be applied when there are conditions that can be used to filter data from specific tables before joining them, thereby reducing the amount of data that needs to be processed during the join operation.
