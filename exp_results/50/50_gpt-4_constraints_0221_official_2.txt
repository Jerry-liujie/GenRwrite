1. Use explicit JOIN syntax instead of comma-separated FROM clause: This rule can be applied when multiple tables are being joined in a query, especially when there are complex join conditions.
2. Move conditions from WHERE clause to ON clause in JOINs: This rule can be applied when there are conditions in the WHERE clause that directly relate to the join operation and can be used to filter rows before the join is performed.
3. Replace subtraction of date keys with direct comparison: This rule can be applied when there are calculations involving date keys in the WHERE clause that can be replaced with direct comparisons for better performance.
