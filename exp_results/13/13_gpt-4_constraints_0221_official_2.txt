1. Use explicit JOIN syntax instead of comma-separated tables in the FROM clause: This rule can be applied when multiple tables are being joined in a query, especially when there are complex join conditions.
2. Move join conditions from WHERE clause to ON clause in JOIN statements: This rule can be applied when join conditions are specified in the WHERE clause of a query instead of the ON clause of a JOIN statement.
3. Remove unnecessary parentheses: This rule can be applied when there are redundant or unnecessary parentheses in the query that do not contribute to the logical structure or readability of the query.
