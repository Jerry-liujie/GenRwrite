2. Move conditions from WHERE clause to ON clause in relevant JOINs: This rule can be applied when there are conditions in the WHERE clause that directly relate to the joining of tables, these conditions can be moved to the ON clause in the relevant JOINs to improve performance.
3. Group related conditions in parentheses: This rule can be applied when there are multiple conditions in the WHERE clause that are related and can be logically grouped together using parentheses to improve readability and potentially optimize the query execution.
4. Remove redundant conditions: This rule can be applied when there are conditions that are repeated in multiple OR clauses in the WHERE clause, these conditions can be moved to a higher level in the WHERE clause to simplify the query and potentially improve performance.
