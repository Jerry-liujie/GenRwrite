1. Use JOIN instead of comma-separated tables in FROM clause: This rule can be applied when multiple tables are being queried together and there are conditions that link these tables.
2. Move join conditions from WHERE clause to ON clause in JOINs: This rule can be applied when there are conditions in the WHERE clause that are used to link tables together.
3. Remove unnecessary parentheses: This rule can be applied when there are parentheses in the query that do not affect the logic of the query and only serve to make the query more complex and harder to read.
