1. Use JOIN instead of comma-separated tables in FROM clause: This rule can be applied when multiple tables are being queried together and there are conditions that link these tables in the WHERE clause.
2. Move join conditions from WHERE clause to ON clause in JOINs: This rule can be applied when there are conditions in the WHERE clause that are used to link tables together.
3. Remove redundant join conditions: This rule can be applied when the same condition is repeated in multiple parts of the WHERE clause.
