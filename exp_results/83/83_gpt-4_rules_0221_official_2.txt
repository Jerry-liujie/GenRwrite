1. Use JOIN instead of WHERE for combining tables: This makes the query more readable and easier to understand. It also can improve performance in some cases.
2. Combine conditions in JOIN ON clause: Instead of having the condition in WHERE clause, it's moved to ON clause in JOIN. This can improve performance as it reduces the amount of data that needs to be processed.
3. Avoid subqueries in WHERE clause: Subqueries in WHERE clause can be expensive as they need to be executed for each row. By moving the condition to the ON clause in JOIN, the subquery is only executed once, improving performance.
