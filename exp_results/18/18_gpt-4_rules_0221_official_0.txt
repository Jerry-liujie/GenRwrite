1. Push down predicate: Move the conditions in the WHERE clause into the FROM clause to reduce the size of the intermediate result set. This can significantly reduce the time and space complexity of the query.
2. Use subquery to filter data: Instead of joining all data from tables and then filtering, filter data in subquery first. This can reduce the amount of data to be joined and improve performance.
3. Remove unnecessary join: If a table is joined in the query but its columns are not used in the SELECT, WHERE, GROUP BY or ORDER BY clauses, remove this table from the join. This can reduce the complexity of the join operation.
