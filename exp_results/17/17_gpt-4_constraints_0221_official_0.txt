1. Use explicit JOIN syntax instead of comma-separated tables in the FROM clause: This rule can be applied when multiple tables are being joined in a query, especially when there are complex join conditions.
2. Move conditions from WHERE clause to ON clause in JOINs: This rule can be applied when there are conditions in the WHERE clause that are related to the tables being joined.
3. Remove unnecessary table aliases: This rule can be applied when table aliases are used in the query but they are not necessary for understanding or they do not improve the readability of the query.
4. Order the JOIN operations based on the selectivity of the join condition: This rule can be applied when there are multiple join operations in a query, and the selectivity of the join conditions can be estimated.
