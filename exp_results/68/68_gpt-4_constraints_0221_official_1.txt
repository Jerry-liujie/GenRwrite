1. Use explicit JOIN syntax instead of comma-separated tables in FROM clause: This rule can be applied when multiple tables are being joined in a query, especially when there are complex join conditions.
2. Move conditions from WHERE clause to ON clause in JOINs where applicable: This rule can be applied when there are conditions in the WHERE clause that are directly related to the tables being joined.
3. Grouping operations should be done after filtering operations: This rule can be applied when a query involves both grouping and filtering operations, and the filtering conditions can significantly reduce the amount of data that needs to be grouped.
