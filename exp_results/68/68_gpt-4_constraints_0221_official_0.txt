1. Use explicit JOIN syntax instead of comma-separated tables in the FROM clause: This rule can be applied when multiple tables are being joined in a query, especially when there are complex relationships between the tables.
2. Move conditions from the WHERE clause to the ON clause in relevant JOINs: This rule can be applied when there are conditions in the WHERE clause that are directly related to the tables being joined.
3. Remove unnecessary table aliases: This rule can be applied when there are table aliases in the query that are not needed for clarity or to avoid ambiguity.
