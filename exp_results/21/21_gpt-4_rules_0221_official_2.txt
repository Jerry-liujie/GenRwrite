1. Use CTE (Common Table Expressions): It simplifies the query and makes it easier to understand. It also allows breaking down complex queries into simpler parts.
2. Use explicit JOINs instead of comma-separated table list: It makes the query more readable and easier to understand. It also helps to avoid accidental cartesian products.
3. Move calculations and conditions from the outer query to the CTE: It reduces the complexity of the main query and improves performance by reducing the amount of data processed in the main query.
