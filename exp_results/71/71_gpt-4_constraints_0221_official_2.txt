1. Use JOIN instead of comma-separated FROM clause: This rule can be applied when there are multiple tables in the FROM clause that are related to each other through some conditions in the WHERE clause.
2. Move WHERE clause conditions to ON clause in JOINs when possible: This rule can be applied when there are conditions in the WHERE clause that directly relate the tables being joined.
3. Use UNION ALL instead of UNION when duplicates are not a concern: This rule can be applied when the query is combining results from multiple SELECT statements and it is not necessary to remove duplicate rows.
