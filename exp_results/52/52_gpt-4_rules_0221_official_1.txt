1. Replace implicit JOINs with explicit JOINs: Explicit JOINs are more readable and less prone to errors due to missing join conditions.
2. Move conditions from WHERE clause to ON clause in JOINs: This can improve performance by reducing the number of rows that need to be joined.
3. Use LIMIT clause to reduce the amount of data returned: This can improve performance by reducing the amount of data that needs to be processed and sent to the client.
