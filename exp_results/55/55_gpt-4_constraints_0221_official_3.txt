1. Use explicit JOIN syntax instead of comma-separated tables in FROM clause: This rule can be applied when multiple tables are being joined in a query, especially when there are complex join conditions.
2. Move conditions from WHERE clause to ON clause in JOINs where possible: This rule can be applied when there are conditions in the WHERE clause that are related to the joined tables and can be used to filter the data before the join operation.
3. Remove unnecessary parentheses: This rule can be applied when there are parentheses in the query that are not needed for specifying the order of operations or for grouping expressions.
