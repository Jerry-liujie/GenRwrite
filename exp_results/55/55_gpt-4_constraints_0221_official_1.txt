1. Use explicit JOIN syntax instead of WHERE clause for joining tables: This rule can be applied when the query involves joining multiple tables and the join conditions are specified in the WHERE clause.
2. Move conditions from WHERE clause to ON clause in JOINs: This rule can be applied when there are conditions in the WHERE clause that are related to the tables being joined.
3. Remove unnecessary parentheses: This rule can be applied when the query contains parentheses that are not necessary for specifying the order of operations.
