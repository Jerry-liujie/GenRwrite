1. Use explicit JOIN syntax instead of comma-separated tables in the FROM clause: This makes the query more readable and easier to maintain. It also helps to avoid accidental cross joins.
2. Move conditions from WHERE clause to ON clause in JOINs when possible: This can improve performance by reducing the number of rows that need to be joined. In this case, the condition `store_sales.ss_item_sk = item.i_item_sk` is moved to the ON clause of the JOIN between `store_sales` and `item`.
