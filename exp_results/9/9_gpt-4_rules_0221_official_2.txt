1. Use of CTE (Common Table Expressions): CTEs are temporary result sets that are defined within the execution scope of a single statement and they can be referred later in that statement. This helps in breaking down complex queries into simpler forms which are easily readable and processed faster.
2. Avoiding multiple subqueries: Multiple subqueries for the same data can significantly slow down performance. By using CTEs, we can avoid multiple subqueries and improve performance.
3. Pre-aggregation: By pre-aggregating the data in the CTEs, we can reduce the amount of data that needs to be processed in the main query. This can significantly improve performance for large datasets.
