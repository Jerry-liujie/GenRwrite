1. Replace LEFT JOIN with EXISTS in subqueries: This rule can be applied when the query is using LEFT JOIN to check for existence of rows in another table, and the columns from the joined table are not used in the SELECT, WHERE or GROUP BY clauses.
2. Remove unnecessary grouping in subqueries: This rule can be applied when the columns in the GROUP BY clause are already unique, meaning the grouping operation is redundant.
3. Filter data as early as possible: This rule can be applied when there are conditions in the WHERE clause that can be moved to an earlier stage of the query, such as into a subquery or JOIN condition, to reduce the amount of data being processed.
