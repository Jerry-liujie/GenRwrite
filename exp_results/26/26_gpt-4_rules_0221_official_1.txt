1. Replace joins with subqueries: This can reduce the number of rows that need to be processed in the join operation, thereby reducing the time and resources required.
2. Filter data as early as possible: By filtering data in subqueries before joining, we can reduce the amount of data that needs to be processed in later stages of the query. This can lead to significant performance improvements.
3. Use IN clause for filtering: The IN clause can be more efficient than multiple OR conditions for filtering, especially when the list of values is large.
